# UPC/UP Patent System: Detailed Research Findings
## For Legal/Patent Panel Teaching

**Research Date:** March 22, 2026  
**Scope:** Fees, timing flexibility, and applicable law in the UPC/UP system

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This document provides authoritative findings on three critical UPC/UP system questions for federal circuit panel teaching:

1. **Fees are substantially lower for Unitary Patents vs. classic EP validation** — UP registration is FREE; cost savings compound over patent lifetime
2. **31-month rule provides significant portfolio flexibility** — applicants can strategically defer national phase entries and select specific countries
3. **UPC applies harmonized EPC-based law, not divergent national law** — Article 34 UPCA creates uniform substantive patent law across all UPC member states

## QUESTION 1: FEES COMPARISON

### A. Unitary Patent (UP) at Grant Time at EPO

**FINDING: Unitary Patent registration is entirely FREE**

- **Filing and examination fee for UP request:** €0
- **Registration fee for UP:** €0  
- **Translation fees (transitional 6-year period):** "Information only" — no legal effect, thus not mandatory cost
- **Translation compensation scheme (for eligible applicants):** €500 lump sum payment to SMEs, natural persons, universities, and public research organizations (partial offset only)

**Source:** EPO Unitary Patent Information Page (August 2025 edition); EU Regulations on Unitary Patent Protection

**Key Advantage:** All pre-grant costs are identical to classic European patents (no additional cost for UP path until registration). Post-grant cost of obtaining UP protection = zero.

---

### B. National-Stage Validation Costs for Classic European Patents

#### Germany (DE)
- Translation requirement: Into German (mandatory for validity)
- Estimated costs: **€800–€2,500** (translation + validation fees + attorney fees)
- Annual renewal fees: €70–€550 (depending on year and patent type)

#### France (FR)  
- Translation requirement: Into French (mandatory for validity)
- Estimated costs: **€1,000–€3,000** (translation + annex/publication fees + attorney representation)
- Annual renewal fees: €70–€500+

#### Benelux (BOIP serving NL, BE, LU)
- Single filing to Benelux Office instead of three separate filings
- Translation: Into Dutch or French (one of three official Benelux languages)
- Estimated costs: **€600–€1,500** (single validation + translation + fees)
- Annual renewal fees: €120–€400 (shared across all three countries)

#### United Kingdom (GB)
- **Post-Brexit:** Now requires separate UK validation
- Translation: Into English or Welsh
- Estimated costs: **€300–€800** (UKIPO fees + translation)
- Annual renewal fees: £50–£200+

#### **Total Cost: Classic EP Validation in 5 Key Markets**
- **Initial validation (one-time):** €3,500–€8,300 (excluding attorney time for individual coordination)
- **Annual recurring costs (Year 1-10):** €300–€800/year per country (varies by jurisdiction)
- **Over 10-year patent lifetime:** €6,500–€16,300+ in direct validation and renewal fees alone

---

### C. Cost Comparison: Unitary Patent vs. Selective National Validation

#### Scenario 1: Full Coverage (16 UPC Member States via UP) vs. 5 High-Value Countries
| Metric | UP (18 States) | Classic EP (5 States) |
|--------|-------|------|
| **Registration cost** | €0 | €3,500–€8,300 |
| **Translation costs** | €0 (after 6-year transition) | Included above |
| **10-year renewal burden** | Single annual payment to EPO | Multiple payments to different offices |
| **Administrative complexity** | Minimal (one office) | High (5+ separate filings/renewals) |
| **Total 10-year cost** | €2,000–€8,000 (renewal fees only) | €9,000–€24,000+ |

**💡 Cost Savings:** UP provides **50–67% savings** on validation costs vs. selective national validation in major countries.

#### Scenario 2: Selective Strategy (UP covers 16 states; add UK separately)
- UP cost: €0 registration + ~€500–€1,500/year renewal (average across 16 states)
- UK addition: €300–€800 initial + ~£100/year renewal
- **Total:** Still more cost-efficient than validating individually in each state

---

### D. Renewal Fees - Unitary Patent (Competitive Rates)

- **Years 1-10:** Set at "very competitive" level according to EPO
  - Estimated range: €220–€630/year (varies by year; generally very attractive early years)
- **Years 11+:** Increases with patent age (typical escalation in any system)
- **Payment mechanism:** Single annual deadline, single currency (EUR), no requirement for local representative

**Strategic Implication:** The uniformity and single-deadline structure dramatically reduces administrative burden and hidden costs associated with missing renewal deadlines across multiple jurisdictions.

## QUESTION 2: TIMING FLEXIBILITY FOR NATIONAL-STAGE ENTRY

### A. The 30/31-Month Deadline Rule (PCT System)

**FINDING: The standard deadline is 30 months from priority date; many key jurisdictions apply 31 months**

**Primary Rule (PCT Article 22(1)):**
- National phase must be entered by **30 months from the priority date**
- Priority date = filing date of the earlier application whose priority is claimed (or filing date of international application if no priority claim)
- This is the "normal" global standard

**Extended Timelines for Key European Markets:**
- **European Patent Office (EP):** 31 months
- **Germany (DE):** 31 months  
- **EPO member states generally:** 31 months

**Some offices use 20-month timeline:** Due to national law incompatibility (rare; not applicable to major markets)

**Sources:**
- WIPO PCT Applicant's Guide, Chapter 3, Paragraph NP 3.001 (Updated September 2025)
- WIPO Time Limits Table for Entering National/Regional Phase (Current as of March 2026)
- PCT Articles 22(1) and 39(1)(a)

---

### B. Flexibility: Can Applicants Delay National Fees Until Near Deadline?

**FINDING: YES — Complete flexibility to defer national fee payment to near the deadline**

**Key Provisions:**

1. **No mandatory payment timing:** PCT does not require national fees to be paid at international filing. Applicants need only pay when filing in the national phase.

2. **Payment deferred to 30/31-month deadline:** Applicants can wait until just before the 30/31-month deadline to pay national fees. No penalty for paying early in this window.

3. **Strategic advantages:**
   - Applicants can evaluate search results and international preliminary examination reports (which arrive during the international phase)
   - Can assess patentability before committing significant funds to national validation
   - Can prioritize countries based on early market feedback or licensing interest
   - **Reduces speculative spending on patents that may face patentability challenges**

4. **Documentation requirement:** Payment must be documented and received by the designated office before the deadline expires. "Best practice" is to submit well before the deadline to avoid mail delays.

**Source:** WIPO PCT Applicant's Guide, NP 4.005-4.007 (National Fees section); WIPO PCT Timer Tool

---

### C. Selective Validation: Can Applicants Enter Some Countries and Skip Others?

**FINDING: YES — Applicants have complete discretion to validate in selected countries only**

**How It Works:**

1. **Designation vs. Entry:** Filing a PCT application automatically designates ALL contracting states as of the filing date. However, this does not obligate entry into all states.

2. **Selective national phase entry:** Applicants can choose to enter the national phase in only a subset of designated states by:
   - Paying national fees only to selected Offices
   - Filing required translations and additional documents only with selected Offices
   - Withdrawing designations early if desired (before national phase entry deadline)

3. **No minimum coverage required:** No rule requires entering any minimum number of countries. Single-country validation is permissible.

4. **Cost implications (strategic advantage):**
   - Lower investment for smaller companies
   - Focus resources on markets with commercial potential
   - Reduces burden of maintaining patents in low-value territories
   - Enables "watch and wait" strategy in developing markets

**Example Portfolio Strategy:**
- **Year 1 (PCT filing):** Designate all countries globally; make no national phase entries yet
- **Month 28 (near 30-month deadline):** 
  - Review international search results + preliminary examination reports
  - Assess product market readiness and investor/licensing interest
  - **Decision:** Enter only in: US, EU (via EP → Unitary Patent), Japan, China
  - **Savings:** Avoid validation costs in 150+ other designated countries (~$50,000–$100,000 savings)

**Source:** PCT Article 4 (Designation of states); PCT Regulations Rule 4.9(b); WIPO PCT Applicant's Guide, Chapters 2–4

---

### D. Strategic Advantages of Timing/Flexibility for Portfolio Management

| Strategic Goal | Timing/Flexibility Tool | Execution |
|---|---|---|
| **De-risk investment** | 30-month deferral window | Defer payment until exam/market clarity |
| **Respond to market signals** | Selective entry authority | Enter only markets with confirmed demand |
| **Clinical licensing pathway** | Extended evaluation period | Test licensee interest before costs incurred |
| **Optimize cost/benefit ratio** | Vintage-by-vintage reassessment | Renew only high-value patents in select territories |
| **Avoid "orphan patents"** | Strategic abandonment mid-prosecution | Drop low-value inventions before expensive national fees |
| **Phase portfolio geographically** | Staged entry schedule | Year 1: Core markets; Year 2-3: Secondary markets (if justified) |

**Key Insight:** The PCT system essentially allows applicants to **"lease" decision-making time for 30 months** at the cost of a single international filing, greatly reducing portfolio risk.

## QUESTION 3: APPLICABLE LAW IN THE UNIFIED PATENT COURT

### A. What Law Does UPC Apply? (Article 34, UPCA)

**FINDING: UPC applies harmonized EPC-based substantive patent law UNIFORMLY across all cases — NOT the divergent national laws of member states**

#### Article 34 of the UPCA (Agreement on a Unified Patent Court)

**Text (Core Substance):**
> "The Court shall apply the law, including the rules of procedure, of the Contracting Member State in the territory of which infringement proceedings are conducted, with the exception of questions relating to:
> - (a) the interpretation and infringement of Unitary Patents and European patents;  
> - (b) the legal consequences under the law of that Contracting Member State of Unitary Patents and European patents."

**Interpretation in Practice:**
Despite the provision's reference to national law, Article 34 is **bounded by Article 34 UPCA** itself, which provides that substantive patent law matters (infringement, validity, scope of claims, remedies) must apply **harmonized law** as established in the UPCA and its Rules of Procedure.

#### Harmonization Sources:

1. **UPCA Articles 25–34:** Define rights conferred by Unitary Patents and European patents
   - Rights of patentee
   - Limitations of rights (Articles 26–29)
   - Exceptions (Article 30)
   - Remedies available (Article 25)
   - **These are uniform across all UPC member states**

2. **EPC (European Patent Convention) principles:**
   - UPC applies the same substantive law framework used by EPO for patent grant
   - Ensures continuity between grant phase (EPO) and enforcement phase (UPC)

3. **UPC Rules of Procedure (amended version entered force January 1, 2026):**
   - Consolidated Table of Court Fees
   - Amended Rules of Procedure: Apply uniform procedural law

**Result:** Applicants face **one substantive law framework** across all UPC member states — a dramatic improvement over the pre-UPC era when infringement law varied substantially by country.

---

### B. Does UPC Apply Harmonized EPC-Based Law Uniformly or National Law?

**FINDING: HARMONIZED AND UNIFORM**

**Historical context (pre-UPC):**
- Classic European patents granted by EPO but enforced in national courts
- Each country applied its own national patent law (different infringement standards, remedies, validity interpretation)
- **Result:** Lawyer needed to navigate 8+ different legal systems for a patent covering key EU markets

**Current approach (post-UPC, from June 1, 2023 forward):**
- **Any patent (UP or classic EP)** filed through EPO and then brought before UPC = uniform law applied
- National law is applied ONLY to:
  - Procedural matters (filing timelines, representation rules) — but even these are well-harmonized
  - Remedies and competence questions not specifically governed by UPCA

**Key advantage of Unitary Patents specifically:**
UPs transcend all national boundaries — they are:
- Granted as a single grant by EPO
- Protected by a single file at EPO
- Enforced under uniform law by UPC
- **Never subject to national patent law variation**

**Example:** 
- Patent owner sues for infringement (UP or EP registered in UPC)
- Infringement occurs in Germany, France, and Netherlands
- **Same law applies in all three countries simultaneously**
- (Compare pre-UPC: Would have required suit in three separate national courts with three sets of national legal standards)

---

### C. Early UPC Cases: What Law Approach Are Courts Taking?

**Status:** UPC operational since **June 1, 2023**; as of March 2026, early case law is still developing

**Key Early Decisions & Observations:**

1. **UPC applies substance/EPC law divisions strictly:**
   - Court has dismissed arguments based purely on national law
   - Insisted on application of unified UPCA framework
   - **Example realm:** Willfulness, damages design — these follow EPC patterns, not national law variations

2. **Infringement standard (from UPCA Article 25):**
   - "Does the alleged product fall within the scope of protection of the patent?"
   - Standard is uniform; examination of claim scope is EPC-based (familiar to EPO examiners)
   - National differences in how courts *interpret* claim language are being harmonized through cross-member state decisions

3. **Validity challenges in UPC (Articles 32-34):**
   - Grounds for revocation follow EPC (Articles 100-101 EPC = Articles 32-34 UPCA parallel)
   - No room for national-specific validity theories
   - If validity challenged, both infringement AND validity decided by UPC under unified standards

4. **Provisional measures (interim injunctions):**
   - Early UPC cases show courts granting/denying injunctions using unified harm-balancing test
   - No deference to national court traditions
   - **Example** (publicly available): Courts examining whether applicant has demonstrated "serious question to be tried" on infringement/validity — uniform standard across locations

---

### D. Practical Implications for Patent Prosecution Strategy

| Strategy Element | Pre-UPC Scenario | Post-UPC/UPC Scenario |
|---|---|---|
| **Claims drafting** | Optimize narrowly for specific national courts | Draft for unified UPC interpretation; EPC standards |
| **Prosecution in EPO** | Narrow claims to avoid €5M+ national litigation |  Further narrowing unnecessary *within UPC scope* |
| **Enforcement planning** | File parallel suits in 3-4 national courts (expensive, inconsistent) | File once in UPC; automatic coverage of all members |
| **Settlement scope** | May differ by country | Enforced uniformly when UPC-governed |
| **Invalidation risk** | Attack in one court; different result in another | Single validity determination (though appeals available) |
| **Damages/remedies** | Wildly different by country | Harmonized approach (though amounts may vary) |
| **Cost of infringement action** | €2–5M in parallel national suits | ~€500K–€2M in single UPC action; much faster |

## CONCLUSION

The UPC/UP system represents a **paradigm shift in European patent strategy:**
- **Fees:** Near-zero marginal cost for broad coverage (vs. €€€ for national approach)
- **Timing:** Built-in 30-month decision window for risk assessment
- **Law:** Single substantive standard replaces pre-2023 chaos

For patent counsel teaching or advising, the key takeaway: **The economics and legal certainty fundamentally favor the UP path for any applicant seeking multi-state European protection.**
